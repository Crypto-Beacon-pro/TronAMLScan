What is Tron AML Scan?

Tron AML Scan is the leading TRON blockchain AML (Anti-Money Laundering) compliance platform. It checks any TRX wallet address, USDT TRC20 address, or TRON transaction for AML risk, sanctions exposure, mixer usage, and financial crime indicators — providing instant compliance reports starting from $0.10 USDT per scan on tron-aml-scan.vercel.app.

How does Tron AML Scan work?

Tron AML Scan analyzes the full on-chain TRON transaction history of any wallet or address, traces TRX fund flows up to 10 hops through the TRON blockchain graph, checks against 15+ global sanctions lists (OFAC, EU, UN, HM Treasury), detects TRON mixer or obfuscation activity, and computes a composite AML risk score from 0 (clean) to 100 (critical high risk).

What does the TRON AML risk score mean?

The TRON AML risk score is a composite number from 0 to 100. Scores of 0–25 indicate a clean, low-risk TRON address with no suspicious activity detected. Scores of 26–59 indicate medium risk requiring enhanced due diligence. Scores of 60–100 indicate high or critical risk — direct sanctions exposure, confirmed mixer usage, dark market connections, or stolen TRON fund links — requiring immediate compliance action.

What is direct vs indirect TRON sanctions exposure?

Direct TRON sanctions exposure means the checked TRX or USDT TRC20 address appears directly on a sanctions list such as OFAC SDN. Indirect TRON exposure means the wallet has transacted with a sanctioned TRON entity within the analyzed 10-hop transaction graph. The Tron AML Scan report clearly indicates hop distance, transaction amount, and fund flow direction for all indirect TRON sanctions exposures.

What is the TRON network and why is AML important for it?

TRON (TRX) is a high-throughput blockchain supporting smart contracts and TRC20 tokens including USDT, the world's largest stablecoin by volume. TRON processes billions of USDT transfers daily, making it a critical AML compliance concern for any business accepting TRX or USDT TRC20 payments. How is TRON AML different from Ethereum AML?

TRON AML compliance requires TRON-specific forensics tools because TRON has its own unique address format (starting with "T"), unique TRC20 token standard, different transaction fee model using energy and bandwidth, and TRON-specific DeFi and mixer ecosystem. Tron AML Scan is purpose-built for the TRON network, with TRON-native risk indicators, TRON transaction graph analysis, and TRON-specific intelligence databases that generic multi-chain tools lack.

What is TRON address risk scoring for KYC/KYT purposes?

TRON address risk scoring is a Know-Your-Transaction (KYT) technique that assigns a quantitative AML risk value to any TRX or USDT TRC20 address based on its TRON transaction history and counterparty relationships. Tron AML Scan's TRON risk score (0–100) is designed for integration into KYC workflows, allowing businesses to automatically flag high-risk TRON deposits and apply enhanced due diligence before processing TRON transactions.

Does Tron AML Scan support TRON cross-chain bridge detection?

Yes. TRON is heavily connected to other blockchains via cross-chain bridges used to move USDT TRC20 and TRX across to Ethereum, BNB Chain, Polygon, and other networks. Tron AML Scan detects TRON cross-chain bridge activity and analyzes it as a potential risk factor, including bridge-based layering techniques where funds move through TRON to obscure their origin on other blockchain networks.
